<p><p>The cavity oscillator market is segmented into two primary types: microwave chambers and radio frequency (RF) cavities. Microwave chambers are designed to generate high-frequency microwave signals, often used in telecommunications and radar systems. In contrast, RF cavities resonate at lower frequencies and are commonly utilized in applications such as particle accelerators and scientific research. Both types play critical roles in various technologies, offering precise signal generation and amplification essential for numerous industrial and scientific applications.</p></p>
