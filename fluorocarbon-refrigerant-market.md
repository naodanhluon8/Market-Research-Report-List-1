<p><p>The fluorocarbon refrigerant market primarily comprises three types: Hydrochlorofluorocarbons (HCFCs), Hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), and Hydrofluoro Olefins (HFOs). HCFCs, once widely used, are being phased out due to their ozone-depleting potential. HFCs, which replaced HCFCs, are potent greenhouse gases but are now facing regulations due to climate change concerns. HFOs, the newer entrants, offer low global warming potential and are considered more environmentally friendly alternatives, gaining traction as sustainable refrigerant solutions.</p></p>
